Executive Summary

Purpose, scope, and system overview

The Waterproofing Activity Management System is a structured digital quality control workflow designed to govern the complete lifecycle of waterproofing operations in multi-storey residential and commercial construction projects. The system enforces sequential execution discipline, multi-party accountability, and full photographic traceability — eliminating the possibility of stage skipping, data loss, or unverified approvals.

The workflow is initiated by the Civil RCC team, who create the activity and populate the Master Data form. This master data — project identity, responsible engineers, drawing references — is automatically inherited by all five downstream stage forms, ensuring zero re-entry and single-source-of-truth data governance. Each stage is assigned to a specific team, who fill one checklist form, and submit it for independent QC review. The next stage unlocks only upon QC approval.

📌 Core Design Principle
This system implements a locked sequential workflow with cascading master data inheritance. No stage can be opened until the prior stage is QC-approved. Every checklist item in every stage form is mandatory in status selection. Comments are conditionally optional on YES responses; photo evidence is conditionally not required on NA responses. All other combinations mandate both.
⚠ Compliance Note
All five QC approval checkpoints constitute formal quality records under the project's Quality Management System (QMS). Rejection at any stage creates a corrective action record that must be closed before progression. The final consolidated output serves as the handover document and is admissible in dispute resolution proceedings.

2.3Data Inheritance Architecture

The Master Data Form (Creation Form) constitutes the authoritative record header for the entire activity. All eight fields from this form are propagated as read-only, immutable values to every stage form. No downstream form can modify master data values. Additionally, each stage's QC approval generates a record (approval date, inspector name, comments) that is appended to the inherited header of all subsequent stage forms.

📐 Inheritance Rule
Stage N Form contains: Master Data (8 fields) + Stage 1..N-1 QC Records + Stage N Checklist (editable). This means Stage 5 carries all prior context and the final QC inspector sees the entire history in one screen.

The accuracy of these cuts — their position, diameter, and the integrity of surrounding concrete — is critical, as they represent permanent penetrations through the waterproofed plane.

      Stage 3 — Surface Preparation & Pre-Ponding Test

      7 checklist items · Filled by Waterproofing Team · QC Review 3

      Stage 3 is the final preparatory stage before the waterproofing membrane is applied. It encompasses surface preparation (laitance removal, bore packing at pipe penetrations, and coving at floor-wall junctions), construction joint treatment, and critically — a minimum 48-hour pre-ponding test at approximately 120mm water depth. This ponding test establishes baseline watertightness of the structural slab before the waterproofing system is layered on top.

          § 8

          Stage 5 — Protection Screed, Post-Ponding & Final Approval

          3 checklist items · Filled by Civil Finish Team · Final QC Review 5

          Stage 5 constitutes the final phase of the waterproofing activity. A cement-sand protection screed (1:3 mix with integral waterproofing admixture, 12–15mm thick) is applied over the cured membrane to prevent physical damage from subsequent construction operations. The screed is laid to a minimum gradient of 1:100 toward drainage points and finished with a broom texture. After a minimum 7-day curing period, a post-ponding test confirms water-tightness of the complete waterproofing system assembly.

            Checklist Field Validation & Conditional Logic

            Business rules governing mandatory fields, conditional requirements, and status-dependent behaviour

            9.1Status Field Rules

            Every checklist item contains three mutually exclusive status fields (YES, NO, NA). Selecting one automatically deselects the others. All three status fields combined are mandatory — one of the three must be selected before form submission is permitted. The table below specifies the conditional behaviour of the Comments and Photo fields based on the selected status.

            Rule ID Status Selected Comments Field Photo Field Rationale
            VR-01 YES Optional — field is active but not required. Placeholder: "Add observation (optional)" Mandatory — photographic evidence of compliance is always required for YES items YES status asserts compliance. Photo provides documentary proof. Comments are optional because the status is self-evidencing.
            VR-02 NO Mandatory — must describe the specific non-conformance observed Mandatory — photographic evidence of the non-compliance is required for corrective action NO status triggers a corrective action. Both the description and visual evidence are required to raise a valid NC record.
            VR-03 N/A Mandatory — must provide justification for why this item is not applicable Not Required — photo field is disabled/greyed; no attachment needed NA must be justified to prevent misuse. Since the item does not apply, physical evidence cannot be captured. Justification text is the control.
            VR-04 Not Selected Blocked — field is inactive until a status is selected Blocked — field is inactive until a status is selected Status selection is the prerequisite. Downstream fields must not be fillable before the primary field is set.
            9.2Form Submission Validation
            📐 Submission Gate Conditions
            A stage form may only be submitted when ALL of the following conditions are simultaneously true:

            (a) Assignment dropdown — a team member is selected
            (b) All checklist items — a status (YES/NO/NA) is selected for every item
            (c) All NO items — Comments field is populated AND Photo is attached
            (d) All NA items — Comments field is populated AND Photo is Not Required
            (e) All YES items — Photo is attached

            If any condition is unmet, the Submit button remains disabled and the unsatisfied fields are highlighted in amber.

            Appendix A — Glossary of Technical Terms

            Definitions of all domain-specific terminology used in this specification

            Annular Gap
            The circular void between a pipe's outer surface and the edge of the hole it passes through in the concrete slab.
            Bore Packing
            Filling the annular gap at pipe penetrations with non-shrink or polymer-modified mortar to eliminate the leak path around the pipe.
            Coving / Fillet
            A triangular mortar formation at floor-wall junctions. Minimum 50×50mm. Allows the membrane to transition from horizontal to vertical without bridging a sharp internal corner.
            Core Cut
            A precision cylindrical hole drilled through a structural slab using a diamond core drilling machine, to accommodate MEP service pipes.
            DFT
            Dry Film Thickness. The thickness of the waterproofing coating after all solvents have evaporated and the film has fully cured. Measured with a DFT gauge.
            Laitance
            A weak, porous layer of cement and fine aggregate particles that rises to the concrete surface during curing. Must be removed before waterproofing to ensure adhesion.
            NC
            Non-Conformance. A formal quality record raised when work does not comply with the project specification, drawing, or approved methodology.
            Plumb
            Perfectly vertical. A wall or column is "in plumb" when it is exactly 90° to the horizontal plane.
            Ponding Test
            A water retention test wherein the floor area is flooded to a specified depth (typically 120mm) and maintained for 48 hours to check for water ingress at the soffit.
            RCC
            Reinforced Cement Concrete. Structural concrete containing steel reinforcement bars (rebars) to resist tensile forces.
            RL
            Reduced Level. A surveyed elevation relative to a datum point, used to ensure correct floor slopes and screed thicknesses.
            Screed
            A thin layer of cement-sand mortar applied over a substrate to provide a level, sloped, or textured working surface. In this context, a protection screed is applied over the waterproofing membrane.
            Snag List
            A quality control punch list of defects or incomplete items identified during inspection that must be rectified before work can be signed off.
            Soffit
            The underside of a floor slab or beam. Damp patches on the soffit during a ponding test indicate water ingress through the slab.
            SSD
            Saturated Surface Dry condition. The concrete surface is internally moist but externally dry — the optimal condition for waterproofing membrane application.
            TDS
            Technical Data Sheet. The product manufacturer's specification document for a material, specifying application method, mix ratios, coverage rates, inter-coat intervals, and curing times.
            Tack-Free
            A curing stage at which the coating surface no longer adheres to a dry finger touch, indicating sufficient solvent evaporation to receive the next coat.
            Upturn
            The vertical extension of the waterproofing membrane up the wall surface from the floor plane. Minimum 300mm required to create a "bathtub" effect preventing lateral water ingress.
